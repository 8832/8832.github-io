County Supervisor Honors Wolf Awareness Week with Proclamation

October 15, 2023

Wolves play a key role in maintaining biodiversity and a strong ecosystem; however, their numbers are dwindling. In response, organizations such as The California Wolf Center have emerged to combat wolf endangerment.

The California Wolf Center, located in rural Julian, is a local nonprofit that acts as an education, conservation, and research facility. Currently home to 25 Mexican Gray wolves, the most endangered wolf subspecies, this center acts with the mission to return wild wolves to their natural habitat.

San Diego County Supervisor Anderson acknowledges The California Wolf Centerâ€™s dedication to protecting and supporting wolves through a proclamation honoring Wolf Awareness Week, a U.S. holiday occurring annually in the third week of October.

Specifically, the theme for the 2023 Wolf Awareness Week was the 50th anniversary of the Endangered Species Act, which allows success for programs working towards species extinctions.

As a representative of the County of San Diego, I was given the opportunity to present the proclamation to the program's executive director, Ms. Kosen, before a crowd of wolf enthusiasts at the California Wolf Center.

Several professional speakers presented after me, and I was able to learn not only from the content that they presented, but also from the way they presented. Their confidence and overall presentation of these professional speakers gave me a reference to learn from.
