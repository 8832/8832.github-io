Reflection

November 28, 2023

As a Mathematics-Computer Science major here at UCSD, I have gained impressive analytical and problem solving skills, and have become well acquainted with technology and mathematics. However, despite pushing my brain to the limits with the complexities surrounding advanced mathematic and computer science courses, I have failed in nurturing and growing skills in communication, and specifically in public speaking.

Entering into the Supervisor Anderson’s internship, I fell into the common category of having glossophobia - the fear of public speaking. Public speaking is not something that is emphasized or considered important in many technology fields, and up until this point, I have had little opportunities, or necessity, to face my fear.

This internship opened the doors to a path which provided the tools and experiences to conquer my fear.

This internship has given me opportunities to speak before crowds: My first experience was to a crowd of around 30, at the California Wolf Center in Julian (October 15, 2023), to present a proclamation and give a few words of support on behalf of the Count of San Diego. My second experience was to a crowd of around 40 at a ribbon cutting ceremony in Poway, where I presented certificates of recognition to a small family business called LUNa’s Lumpias.

In addition to working in a professional environment in the East County Office, I have staffed networking events such as District 2’s Heroes Month Recognition event, and Jumul’s annual Fall Parade (October 2023). These events allowed me to meet and interact with fellow District 2 constituents from a diverse range of ages, backgrounds, and walks of life.

Lastly, with this Academic Internship Project, I have combined the computer science aspect of my UCSD major with several of my experiences from the internship with this website. I self-taught myself HTML and CSS in order to code and design this website, and the contents are reflections on major events I participated in while at this internship.
